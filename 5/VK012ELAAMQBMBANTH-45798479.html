ESATA is actually an external SATA II specification, an extension of the industry standard interface Serial ATA(SATA).
The transmission rate provided by the SATA1.0 specification in 2003 has reached 150MB/s, which is not only higher than the 100MB/s(ATA100) provided by the normal IDE hard disk, but also exceeds the maximum transmission rate of 133MB/s(ATA133).
SATA has also made significant improvements in data reliability. SATA but for the instruction and data packet cyclic redundancy check(CRC), not only can detect all single bit and bit error, and according to the principle of statistics, it also can detect the possible error 99.998%.
In addition to the transmission speed and reliability of transmission data, the saving space is the most attractive part of SATA, which is more conducive to the internal heat dissipation of the chassis, and the crosstalk between cables is also effectively controlled.
